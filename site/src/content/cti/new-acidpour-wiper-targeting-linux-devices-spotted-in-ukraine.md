<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-small-font-size">By: <a href="https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/profile/kevin-poireault/">Kevin Poireault</a> Reporter, Infosecurity Magazine</h3>



<p>A new variant of the wiper malware AcidRain, known as AcidPour, has been discovered by SentinelOne’s threat intelligence team, SentinelLabs.</p>



<p>AcidRain is destructive wiper malware&nbsp;<a href="https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/news-features/role-states-attributing/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">attributed to Russian military intelligence</a>.</p>



<p>In May 2022, AcidRain was used in a broad-scale cyber-attack against Viasat’s KA-SAT satellites in Ukraine.</p>



<p>The malware rendered KA-SAT modems inoperative in Ukraine and caused additional disruptions throughout Europe at the onset of the Russian invasion.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>AcidPour Shows Proximity with AcidRain</strong></h2>



<p>On March 16, 2024, SentinelLabs researchers Juan Andrés Guerrero-Saade and Tom Hegel began&nbsp; observing a suspicious Linux binary uploaded from Ukraine.<br><br>They quickly realized that this activity showed surface similarities with malicious activities originating from AcidRain.</p>



<p>The new malware they observed also showed behaviors similar to AcidRain’s, such as targeting specific directories and device paths common in embedded Linux distributions.</p>
