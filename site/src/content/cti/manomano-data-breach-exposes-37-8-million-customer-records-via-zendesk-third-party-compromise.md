<p>European home improvement marketplace <strong>ManoMano</strong> has confirmed a massive data breach affecting <strong>37.8 million customer accounts</strong> after hackers compromised a third-party customer service provider. The breach, which surfaced on cybercriminal forum BreachForums, represents one of the largest retail data exposures of 2026.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Breach: Third-Party Call Center Compromised</h2>



<p>A threat actor using the handle &#8220;Indra&#8221; posted the stolen data on BreachForums, claiming to have exfiltrated <strong>approximately 43 GB of data</strong> extracted from <strong>Zendesk</strong>, ManoMano&#8217;s customer support platform. The haul includes:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>37.8 million user account records</strong></li>
<li><strong>935,000 after-sales service tickets</strong></li>
<li><strong>More than 13,500 attachments</strong></li>
<li>Customer data spanning <strong>France, Spain, Italy, United Kingdom, and Germany</strong></li>
</ul>



<p>The attack vector traced back to a <strong>third-party call center based in Tunisia</strong>. Attackers reportedly compromised a workstation belonging to one of the service agents, gaining access to the Zendesk backend that ManoMano uses to manage customer interactions.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Crisis Response: Emergency Relocation to Madagascar</h2>



<p>Following the breach, ManoMano was forced to execute an <strong>emergency repatriation of its entire customer service department</strong> to ADM Value in Madagascar. The company also engaged <strong>Burson</strong>, a renowned press agency, to manage communications around the incident.</p>



<p>While ManoMano officially acknowledged only &#8220;thousands of customers&#8221; affected in its public statements, the evidence posted by the threat actor suggests the actual scope is <strong>orders of magnitude larger</strong>.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Why This Matters</h2>



<p>This breach highlights several critical security concerns:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Third-Party Risk:</strong> ManoMano&#8217;s security posture was ultimately undermined by a vendor&#8217;s compromised workstation, demonstrating how supply chain security extends to every contractor handling customer data.</li>
<li><strong>Zendesk as an Attack Vector:</strong> Customer service platforms like Zendesk aggregate vast amounts of sensitive information, making them high-value targets for attackers.</li>
<li><strong>Cross-Border Data Impact:</strong> With customer records spanning five European countries, this breach triggers complex regulatory obligations under GDPR, potentially exposing ManoMano to significant fines.</li>
<li><strong>Transparency Gap:</strong> The disparity between ManoMano&#8217;s public acknowledgment (&#8220;thousands&#8221;) and the actual scope (37.8 million) raises questions about corporate breach disclosure practices.</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What Exposed Data Could Enable</h2>



<p>The stolen data creates significant risks for affected customers:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Targeted Phishing:</strong> Attackers can craft convincing phishing campaigns using purchase history and support ticket context</li>
<li><strong>Identity Fraud:</strong> Personal details combined with purchase information provide fodder for social engineering</li>
<li><strong>Credential Stuffing:</strong> Email addresses can be cross-referenced against other breaches for credential reuse attacks</li>
<li><strong>Warranty/Return Fraud:</strong> Support ticket attachments may contain order details enabling fraudulent claims</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Action Items</h2>



<p>Organizations should take this breach as a wake-up call to:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Audit Third-Party Access:</strong> Review all vendors with access to customer data platforms</li>
<li><strong>Implement Zero-Trust for Support Systems:</strong> Apply least-privilege access controls to customer service tools</li>
<li><strong>Monitor Dark Web:</strong> Proactively scan for company and customer data on cybercriminal forums</li>
<li><strong>Strengthen Vendor Security Requirements:</strong> Mandate security standards for offshore service providers</li>
</ul>



<p>ManoMano, founded in 2013 and valued as a French unicorn startup, operates in six European markets. This incident follows previous controversies around the company&#8217;s &#8220;Manodvisors&#8221; program, where customers were allegedly incentivized to write positive reviews.</p>
