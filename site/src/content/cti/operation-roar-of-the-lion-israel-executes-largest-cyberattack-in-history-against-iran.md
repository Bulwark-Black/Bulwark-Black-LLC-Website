<p>In an unprecedented display of cyber warfare capability, <strong>Israel has executed what is being described as the largest cyberattack in history</strong>, plunging Iran into near-total digital darkness during a coordinated military operation on Saturday, February 28, 2026.</p>
<h2>Near-Total Internet Blackout Confirmed</h2>
<p>Independent internet monitor <a href="https://twitter.com/netblocks" target="_blank" rel="noopener">NetBlocks</a> confirmed that Iran&#8217;s national connectivity dropped to <strong>just 4% of normal levels</strong>—effectively a complete nationwide shutdown. Cloudflare corroborated the data, showing internet traffic in the country falling to near-zero by 18:45 UTC (22:15 local time).</p>
<p>The cyberattack accompanied <strong>Operation &#8220;Roar of the Lion,&#8221;</strong> a joint US-Israeli military strike targeting IRGC command centers across Iran.</p>
<h2>Critical Infrastructure Targeted</h2>
<p>Western intelligence sources indicated the cyber operation was designed to:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Disrupt IRGC communications infrastructure</strong> to prevent coordination of counterattacks</li>
<li><strong>Disable drone and ballistic missile launch capabilities</strong> by targeting Iranian cyber and electronic units</li>
<li><strong>Neutralize regime propaganda</strong>—the IRNA news agency website was taken offline, while Tasnim (IRGC-affiliated) experienced severe disruptions and displayed subversive messages against Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei</li>
<li><strong>Target energy and aviation infrastructure</strong> through deep intrusions into critical data systems</li>
</ul>
<p>Reports from Tehran, Isfahan, and Shiraz described widespread failures of local apps and government digital services. Even Iran&#8217;s isolated &#8220;national internet&#8221; network—designed to operate independently from the global internet—reportedly collapsed under the combined offensive.</p>
<h2>Multi-Domain Attack</h2>
<p>The operation combined multiple attack vectors:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Electronic warfare</strong> disrupting navigation and communications systems</li>
<li><strong>Distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks</strong> against public-facing infrastructure</li>
<li><strong>Deep network intrusions</strong> targeting energy and aviation systems</li>
<li><strong>Information operations</strong> hijacking state media to broadcast anti-regime messages</li>
</ul>
<p>This assault represents the culmination of a campaign that began in January 2026, when government satellite broadcasts were hacked to air content calling for the regime&#8217;s overthrow to millions of Iranian households.</p>
<h2>Why It Matters</h2>
<p>This operation demonstrates several critical developments in modern cyber warfare:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cyber operations as force multipliers:</strong> By blinding Iran&#8217;s command and control during kinetic strikes, the attackers significantly degraded defensive response capabilities</li>
<li><strong>Air-gapped systems are not immune:</strong> Even Iran&#8217;s isolated national internet could not withstand the coordinated assault</li>
<li><strong>State-level cyber capabilities continue advancing:</strong> The scale and coordination of this attack represents a new benchmark in offensive cyber operations</li>
<li><strong>Information warfare integration:</strong> Combining infrastructure attacks with propaganda hijacking creates psychological effects beyond the technical damage</li>
</ul>
<h2>Implications for Critical Infrastructure Security</h2>
<p>Organizations worldwide should take note: if a nation-state&#8217;s isolated networks can be compromised during a coordinated attack, no organization is immune. This event underscores the importance of:</p>
<ul>
<li>Defense-in-depth strategies that assume breach</li>
<li>Out-of-band communication capabilities</li>
<li>Resilience planning for total connectivity loss</li>
<li>Threat intelligence monitoring for geopolitical escalation that may impact your sector</li>
</ul>
