<p>A major ransomware attack on BridgePay Network Solutions has caused a nationwide payment processing outage, forcing merchants across the United States to switch to cash-only operations and disrupting card transactions for municipalities and businesses alike.</p>
<h2>Ransomware Confirmed Within Hours</h2>
<p>BridgePay confirmed late Friday, February 6, 2026, that ransomware was responsible for the incident that began in the early morning hours. The company has engaged federal law enforcement, including the <strong>FBI and U.S. Secret Service</strong>, along with external forensic and recovery teams.</p>
<p>&#8220;Initial forensic findings indicate that no payment card data has been compromised,&#8221; BridgePay stated in an update on their status page, adding that any accessed files were encrypted and there is currently &#8220;no evidence of usable data exposure.&#8221;</p>
<h2>Merchants Report Widespread Disruption</h2>
<p>As the outage spread, merchants and organizations across the country began informing customers they could only accept cash payments. Restaurants, retail stores, and municipal offices were all affected by the disruption.</p>
<p>The <strong>City of Palm Bay, Florida</strong> announced: &#8220;BridgePay Network Solutions, our third-party credit card processing vendor, is experiencing a nationwide service disruption. As a result, the City&#8217;s online billing payment portal is currently unavailable.&#8221;</p>
<p>Other impacted organizations include:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Lightspeed Commerce</strong> — reported service impacts affecting their payment integrations</li>
<li><strong>ThriftTrac</strong> — experienced processing disruptions</li>
<li><strong>City of Frisco, Texas</strong> — reported municipal payment issues</li>
</ul>
<h2>Critical Systems Taken Offline</h2>
<p>BridgePay&#8217;s status page showed major outages across core production systems, including:</p>
<ul>
<li>BridgePay Gateway API (BridgeComm)</li>
<li>PayGuardian Cloud API</li>
<li>MyBridgePay virtual terminal and reporting</li>
<li>Hosted payment pages</li>
<li>PathwayLink gateway and boarding portals</li>
</ul>
<p>Early warning signs appeared around 3:29 a.m. when monitoring detected degraded performance across multiple services. The intermittent degradation eventually cascaded into a full system outage.</p>
<h2>Why This Matters</h2>
<p>This attack highlights the <strong>cascading impact</strong> of ransomware targeting payment infrastructure. Unlike attacks on individual businesses, striking a payment gateway creates immediate, widespread real-world consequences:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Business continuity:</strong> Merchants unable to process cards lose revenue and customer trust</li>
<li><strong>Municipal services:</strong> Government offices cannot collect utility payments or process transactions</li>
<li><strong>Supply chain ripple:</strong> Third-party integrators like Lightspeed Commerce face downstream impacts</li>
<li><strong>Consumer inconvenience:</strong> Customers forced to find cash in an increasingly cashless economy</li>
</ul>
<p>The ransomware group responsible has not been publicly identified. BridgePay stated that recovery is being handled &#8220;in a secure and responsible manner&#8221; while the forensic investigation continues.</p>
<p>This incident adds to the growing wave of ransomware attacks targeting critical payment infrastructure, where outages quickly ripple through real-world commerce when transaction pipelines go down.</p>
